What is the difference between structured content and semantic HTML?

Structured content describes the way or methods any particular clust of data or information
is found by algorithms. To be a bit more specific, it is one of the first things that
that AI voice assistants tend to look for to help a particular relating item to a consumers
inquiry, or key collection of terms that organize needed information to be grouped up
to be displayed immediately. On the other end, semantic HTML also describes the way information
is displayed, however, it makes more emphasis on the webpage itself on how it visually presents
itself to allow a meaningful relationship with the consumer who is viewing it. It can often
aid consumers to locate particular things in sections and different areas on a website easily,
given that each element in HTML is clearly split up

How do they work together?

Often when AI voice assitants or algorithms try to find inquries for a consumer to look at, the search
engine will pull up links based on key words or organized clusters of key information needed to satisfy
the consumer. Some of these would include a specific list of songs that were listed as most popular on a website.
HTML would list those songs in a very particular way in code that it makes it very visually obvious where the list is.
Structured content would allow algorithims to then find that list and allow that website to be one of the first sources
to pop up as results given the key words and content.

Why is it important to have structured content and semantic HTML in your web pages?

Often times there will be a mixup with AI voice assistants where the search will not result in the particular answer
that you require because of the way that structured content may prioritize key words that have particular qualities
in the code. This is why both are needed. A poorly designed website with visually unappealing organization will mess
with the structured content, given there is a mess of what to focus on, search engines will struggle to show the quick
answers that a user needs. Having a website having good design will for one allow a consumer to find things easier, but
also allow optimization for search engines to know what is important to focus on when results are being pulled up or what
particular cluster of information gets intially displayed without the full website being open
